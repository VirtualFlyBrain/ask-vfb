Functional Hypotheses

high

Convergent looming computation: LC4 and LPLC2 provide parallel, complementary looming signals to the GF. LC4 encodes expanding edges (size-velocity), while LPLC2 computes radial optic flow (motion-based). Their convergence on GF (~4,648 combined synapses) implements an AND-like gate requiring both feature types for reliable looming detection, reducing false positives from non-looming stimuli.

Evidence: von Reyn et al. 2017, Ache et al. 2019, Hemibrain connectivity data
medium

Inhibitory gain control via PVLP interneurons: GABAergic (PVLP011) and glutamatergic/GABAergic (PVLP010) interneurons receive input from LC4/LPLC2 and inhibit the GF. This creates a feedforward inhibition motif that raises the GF's firing threshold, ensuring escape is only triggered by strong, sustained looming signals. This may implement a size-threshold mechanism where escape is triggered only when the approaching object reaches a critical angular size.

Evidence: Hemibrain connectivity (245 + 50 inhibitory synapses onto GF), Fotowat et al. 2009 (threshold-based escape)
medium

Parallel escape pathways: LC4 and LPLC2 both project to DNp04, a separate descending neuron, in addition to the GF. This suggests a dual-pathway architecture where the GF mediates fast, stereotyped escape jumps (via electrical synapses to TTMn), while DNp04 may drive slower, more flexible escape behaviors such as directional wing-based takeoff.

Evidence: Hemibrain connectivity (LC4→DNp04: 58 syn, LPLC2→DNp04: 24 syn), Card 2012 (multiple escape strategies)
high

Electrical synapses ensure speed and reliability: The GF→TTMn and GF→PSI connections use gap junctions (ShakB/Innexin-dependent), not chemical synapses. This enables sub-millisecond transmission latency, critical for the ~5ms brain-to-muscle escape response. The electrical coupling also makes transmission virtually failure-proof, unlike chemical synapses that can undergo short-term depression.

Evidence: King and Wyman 1980, Allen et al. 1998, Phelan et al. 1996 (ShakB mutant analysis)
low

Lateral excitation between LC4 and LPLC2 may sharpen looming selectivity: The small reciprocal connections between LC4 and LPLC2 (~15-21 synapses) could provide positive feedback during genuine looming stimuli, amplifying the combined signal to GF when both pathways are co-activated by a true approaching object.

Evidence: Hemibrain connectivity (lateral connections), Hypothetical — functional significance not directly tested
